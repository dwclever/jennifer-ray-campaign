Rooted in Community, Dedicated to Putnam County

A Small-Town Foundation

My story begins in the small, lakeside hamlet of Sandy Pond, New York. Growing up in a town with just one traffic light and a graduating class of only 55 people, I learned early on that in a small community, your neighbors are your lifeline. Whether it was borrowing an egg for a Sunday cake or a neighbor stopping by with a helping hand when someone was under the weather, we looked out for one another.

I grew up in a world where Kindergarten through 12th grade was all in one building. That experience gave me a profound appreciation for the importance of a tight-knit school system and the way a community rallies around its youth. Just like here in Putnam County, my childhood home was defined by hard-working dairy farms and the quiet strength of the lake. It was a place where hard work was expected, and a person's word was their bond.

Choosing Georgia, Loving Putnam

In 2002, I made the best decision of my life when I moved to Georgia. It was here that I met my husband, Charles Ray—a Georgia native who grew up in Fitzgerald. We started our life together in 2003 in a modest two-bedroom cottage on Burton Road; we didn't have much space, but we had a home we loved and a community that welcomed us with open arms.

Over the last two decades, I have spent more of my adult life here than anywhere else. While I didn't have a choice in where I was born, I made a conscious, heartfelt choice to make Putnam County my home. I have watched this county grow, I have shared in its challenges, and I feel profoundly blessed to call this place my own.

Why I'm Running for Commissioner

I am running for County Commissioner because I believe the values I was raised with—neighbor helping neighbor, fiscal responsibility, and local pride—are exactly what we need to protect our future.

I may not have been born in Georgia, but my heart, my home, and my commitment belong to Putnam County. I care deeply about our shared future, and as your Commissioner, I will work tirelessly to represent every resident with the same small-town integrity that has guided me my entire life.

Putnam County is more than where I live—it's who I am.
